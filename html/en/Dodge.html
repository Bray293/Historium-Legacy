DODGE

The Early Years of Dodge
(1910s)

Dodge was founded in 1900 by brothers John and Horace Dodge in Detroit, Michigan. Initially, the company manufactured parts for other automakers, notably Ford. In 1914, Dodge produced its first car, the Dodge Model 30, which stood out for its robust design and performance. This car marked the beginning of vehicle production under the Dodge Brothers name.

Thanks to the quality of their vehicles, Dodge quickly became a major player in the American automotive industry, competing with Ford and General Motors.

The 1920s and 1930s
Innovation and Success

After the death of the Dodge brothers in 1920, the company continued to prosper under new ownership. During the 1920s and 1930s, Dodge innovated with more powerful vehicles, like the Dodge Series 116, while diversifying into truck production.

The brand also experienced rapid growth following its acquisition by Chrysler in 1928, integrating Dodge into the portfolio of one of America's largest automakers.

Post-War and Rising Power
(1940s-1960s)

After World War II, Dodge solidified its position as a utility vehicle manufacturer with models like the Dodge Power Wagon (1946), which became a massive success as a sturdy and reliable truck. During the 1950s, Dodge also produced several iconic passenger cars, including the Dodge Coronet and the Dodge Royal.

These models helped Dodge stand out with their combination of performance and comfort.

The Muscle Cars
(1960s-1970s)

In the 1960s, Dodge became one of the leaders in the "muscle car" segment with legendary models like the Dodge Charger and Dodge Challenger. The Charger, particularly in its Daytona version, became an icon due to its racing success and aggressive design.

The Dodge Challenger, launched in 1970, marked the pinnacle of muscle cars, offering powerful engines and distinctive styling, becoming a favorite among performance car enthusiasts.

The Modern Era
(1980s-2000s)

In the 1980s, Dodge experienced a renaissance with vehicles like the Dodge Ram, which became one of the best-selling pickups in the U.S. The company also diversified its range with models like the Dodge Caravan, one of the first minivans, and the Viper, a supercar that redefined performance standards.

With these models, Dodge managed to establish a strong presence in several segments while maintaining its performance spirit.

The Contemporary Era
(2010 to Present)

Since the 2010s, Dodge has continued to focus on extreme performance with models like the Dodge Challenger SRT Hellcat, one of the most powerful cars on the market. The brand has also strengthened its range of utility vehicles with modernized versions of the Dodge Ram, now a standalone brand.

At the same time, Dodge remains true to its muscle car roots while embracing modern technologies to stay competitive in an ever-evolving market.
