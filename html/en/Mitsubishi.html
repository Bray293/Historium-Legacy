MITSUBISHI

The Beginnings of Mitsubishi
(1870s-1910)

Mitsubishi was founded in 1870 by Yataro Iwasaki under the name **Mitsubishi Shokai**, a maritime transportation company. The company later diversified its activities to include sectors such as manufacturing, shipbuilding, and automotive. In 1917, Mitsubishi launched the **Mitsubishi Model A**, the first mass-produced car in Japan, marking its entry into the automotive industry.

The name Mitsubishi comes from the Japanese words "Mitsu" (three) and "Hishi" (diamond), referring to the company's famous logo representing three diamonds.

Industrial Expansion
(1920s-1940)

In the 1920s, Mitsubishi diversified its activities, becoming a conglomerate involved in heavy industrial production, including aircraft, ships, and military vehicles. During World War II, Mitsubishi played a key role in producing military aircraft, notably the famous **Mitsubishi A6M Zero**.

After the war, the company reorganized to focus more on automotive and civil industrial production.

Post-War Revival
(1950s-1960s)

After World War II, Mitsubishi refocused its efforts on automotive production. In 1960, it launched the **Mitsubishi 500**, its first successful passenger car. This model marked the beginning of a new era for Mitsubishi Motors, with a clear focus on producing cars for the general public.

In the 1960s, Mitsubishi also expanded its exports internationally, becoming a major player in the global market.

Success in Motorsport
(1970s-1980s)

Mitsubishi achieved great success in motorsport, particularly with the **Lancer Evolution**, an iconic car that dominated rallies in the 1980s and 1990s. Mitsubishi's technological innovations, such as all-wheel drive and turbocharged engines, allowed the brand to stand out in automotive competitions.

The **Lancer Evo** became a symbol of performance and reliability, gaining fans worldwide.

SUV and 4x4 Domination
(1980s-1990s)

Mitsubishi also distinguished itself in the SUV segment with the launch of the **Mitsubishi Pajero** in the 1980s. This rugged off-road vehicle quickly became a reference for 4x4 enthusiasts and won several victories in the **Dakar Rally**, solidifying Mitsubishi's reputation in the off-road vehicle segment.

The Pajero, with its competition success and commercial popularity, became one of Mitsubishi's flagship models.

Environmental Innovations
(2000s)

In the 2000s, Mitsubishi began to focus on more environmentally friendly technologies. The launch of the **Mitsubishi i-MiEV** in 2009, one of the first electric cars for the general public, demonstrated the brand's commitment to ecological innovation.

Mitsubishi also invested in hybrid technologies and continued to develop fuel-efficient vehicles, adapting to the growing demands for sustainability in the automotive industry.

Hybridization and Future
(2010-2020)

The **Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV**, launched in 2013, became one of the most popular plug-in hybrid SUVs in the world, combining the versatility of an SUV with the energy efficiency of a hybrid. This model marked a turning point in Mitsubishi's approach to sustainable development.

Looking ahead, Mitsubishi continues to focus on electrification and innovation while maintaining its values of robustness and performance.

Increased Electrification and Future
(2020 - Present)

Since 2020, Mitsubishi has accelerated its commitment to electrification, particularly with the plug-in hybrid version of the **Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross** in 2021. This SUV combines plug-in hybrid technology with a modern design and advanced features, meeting the growing demand for more eco-friendly vehicles.

In parallel, Mitsubishi continues to develop new electric and hybrid models while focusing on technology, safety, and performance. Mitsubishi's strategy for the future focuses on sustainable mobility while maintaining its reputation for robust and reliable vehicles.

Mitsubishi plans to launch new all-electric and hybrid models as part of its growth plan aimed at reducing CO2 emissions, particularly in key markets like Europe and Asia.
