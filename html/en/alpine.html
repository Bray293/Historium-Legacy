ALPINE

The Beginnings of Alpine
(1950s-1960s)

Alpine was founded in 1955 by Jean Rédélé, a passionate rally driver. His first creation, the Alpine A106, was a lightweight coupe based on the Renault 4CV's mechanics, designed for exceptional performance. Alpine quickly made a name for itself in the racing world thanks to its agile, lightweight cars suited for rallies.

Alpine gained fame after victories in various competitions, and the brand earned a strong reputation for its sporty and innovative spirit, reaching new heights with the iconic Alpine A110, launched in 1962, which soon became a legend.

Golden Years and Racing Success
(1960s-1970s)

The 1960s and 1970s marked the golden era of Alpine, especially with the A110, which dominated international rally competitions. In 1973, Alpine won the first-ever World Rally Championship for manufacturers, solidifying its legendary status in the automotive world.

With the creation of Alpine Renault in 1973, the collaboration with Renault grew stronger, enabling Alpine to benefit from greater production capacity while maintaining its core focus on performance. The Alpine A310 was launched during this period, representing a new generation of sports cars.

Challenges of the 1980s
(1980s)

In the 1980s, Alpine faced commercial challenges and a shifting automotive market. Despite this, the brand continued to produce iconic models like the Alpine GTA, which combined performance with comfort to appeal to a broader audience.

Unfortunately, in 1995, facing increased competition and financial difficulties, Renault decided to cease Alpine production. The brand disappeared from the automotive landscape, though its sporty spirit and legacy continued to inspire car enthusiasts.

The Great Alpine Comeback
(2010 - Present)

After several decades of absence, Renault announced Alpine's revival in 2012. In 2017, Alpine returned to the spotlight with the new Alpine A110, a modern reinterpretation of the legendary 1960s A110. This model was an immediate success, praised for its lightness, agility, and design that paid tribute to the brand's heritage.

Since its comeback, Alpine has reestablished itself as a premium brand in the sports car segment. The brand’s commitment to motorsport continues, particularly at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, where it has delivered remarkable performances. Today, Alpine embodies innovation and excellence, while remaining true to its roots as a manufacturer of lightweight, high-performance sports cars.
