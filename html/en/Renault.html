RENAULT

The Beginnings of Renault
(1899-1910)

Renault was founded in 1899 by Louis Renault and his brothers. The first car, the Renault Voiturette, is an immediate success, allowing the company to quickly gain recognition for its quality and innovation. Louis Renault, passionate about mechanics, revolutionizes the industry with his avant-garde designs.

The brand also participates in automobile races, such as the Paris-Madrid of 1903, reinforcing its reputation and image of performance. In a short time, Renault becomes one of the leading automobile manufacturers in France.

Expansion and Innovation
(1910-1930)

In the 1920s, Renault continues to innovate with iconic models such as the Renault NN and the Renault 40 CV. The introduction of the assembly line allows Renault to reduce costs and increase production, making its vehicles more accessible to a wider audience.

Renault also diversifies by starting to produce trucks and military vehicles during World War I, playing a key role in the war effort. This diversification lays the groundwork for a varied portfolio that will continue to grow in the following decades.

Success After the War
(1940-1960)

After World War II, Renault resumes production with the 4CV, which becomes a symbol of French reconstruction. This economical and practical model achieves immense success, selling over 1 million units.

In the 1960s, Renault expands internationally, establishing partnerships with foreign manufacturers and opening factories abroad. The brand also participates in the creation of the Renault 8, which illustrates the shift towards a family car that is practical and accessible.

Renault and Diversification
(1970-1980)

The 1970s and 1980s see Renault expanding its range of models with popular cars like the Renault 5 and the Renault 9. These models attract a wide clientele and reinforce the brand's position in the European market.

Renault also begins to explore more sporty market segments with models like the Renault Alpine, which distinguishes itself in the world of rallies and races, winning several championships and thus reinforcing its dynamic and innovative brand image.

The Clio Years and Modernization
(1990-2000)

In the 1990s, the Renault Clio becomes one of the best-selling models in Europe, embodying versatility and modern design. This model stands out for its comfort, safety, and value for money, attracting a wide audience.

The brand also invests in new technologies, including the development of more fuel-efficient and less polluting engines. Renault commits to research and development of low-emission cars and, later, hybrid vehicles.

Innovations and Sustainable Commitment
(2010 - Present)

Renault has doubled its efforts to position itself as a leader in sustainable mobility, with the launch of the ZOE in 2012, which has become the best-selling electric car in Europe. The company has strengthened its alliance with Nissan and Mitsubishi to develop a broader and more innovative range of electric vehicles.

The Talisman and Captur, which have seen great commercial success, are examples of Renault's commitment to offering vehicles that meet the needs of modern consumers.
