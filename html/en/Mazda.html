MAZDA

The early years of Mazda
(1920s-1960s)

Mazda was founded in 1920 as Toyo Cork Kogyo Co., Ltd. The company started with industrial products before moving into automobile production. In 1960, Mazda launched its first passenger car, the **Mazda R360**, a microcar that revolutionized the Japanese market with its lightweight design and affordable price.

The name Mazda comes from Ahura Mazda, the god of wisdom in Persian culture, symbolizing the brand's pursuit of innovation and wisdom.

The introduction of the rotary engine
(1960s-1970s)

In 1967, Mazda introduced the **Mazda Cosmo Sport 110S**, the world's first production car equipped with a Wankel rotary engine. This innovative engine became a Mazda hallmark, allowing the company to stand out from other automakers with its efficiency and performance.

This technology was further refined in subsequent years, especially with the release of the **Mazda RX-7** in 1978, an iconic sports car powered by a rotary engine.

The golden age of rotary engines
(1970s-1980s)

The **Mazda RX-7**, launched in 1978, marked the peak of the Wankel rotary engine. This sporty coupe gained popularity worldwide, particularly for its impressive performance and sleek design. Mazda strengthened its image as a maker of passionate, innovative, and performance-oriented cars.

During this period, Mazda also excelled in motorsport, securing significant victories with its rotary-powered models.

The MX-5 Miata icon
(1980s-1990s)

In 1989, Mazda introduced the **Mazda MX-5 Miata**, a lightweight roadster inspired by British sports cars. The MX-5 quickly became a global benchmark for driving pleasure, thanks to its perfect balance of agility, performance, and accessibility. This model is now the best-selling roadster of all time.

The end of the rotary engine
(1990s-2000s)

Although the rotary engine was Mazda's signature, the 2000s marked the end of its widespread use. The **Mazda RX-8** (2003) was the last model to be equipped with this technology. Despite its innovation, the rotary engine suffered from fuel consumption and emissions issues, pushing Mazda to turn to more efficient technologies.

Nonetheless, the RX-8 remained an iconic car, offering a unique driving experience.

SKYACTIV innovations
(2010s)

In the 2010s, Mazda introduced **SKYACTIV** technology, a set of mechanical innovations aimed at improving fuel efficiency while maintaining dynamic performance. This technology was featured in models like the **Mazda 3** and **Mazda CX-5**, which won numerous awards for their design, safety, and driving pleasure.

Mazda proved that efficiency and driving fun can go hand in hand.

Electrification and the future
(2020s)

In 2020, Mazda launched its first electric vehicle, the **Mazda MX-30**, a compact SUV with an elegant design. This model marks Mazda's entry into the electrification era, while maintaining the brand's values in terms of design and driving pleasure.
